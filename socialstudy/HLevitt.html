World History (Honors & AP)

Mrs. Hannah Levitt

Teacher Response

What students will learn:

Modern world history from 1200 C.E. to the 1990s. Students will also learn historical reasoning skills such as causation, contextualization, comparison, and continuity/change. Students will learn how to write DBQs, LEQs, and SAQs.

In-class Workload:

The class is divided between direct instruction and project-based learning. Homework consists of reading and writing assignments. Students are also encouraged to watch Heimler's History and Crash Course World History.

Homework:

Once or twice a week, articles and other reading/writing assignments are given that don't take much time.

Materials Required:

A binder, color pencils, highlighters, pencils, pens.

Tests & Quizzes:

Progress checks are given on every unit. Four tests are assigned by time periods (including the midterm and final).

Self-Studying:

Yes, the students are prepared to get at least 3 on the AP exam. If students want to get a 4 or 5 they must study outside of class.

If your class is AP, please describe the AP exam.

55 multiple choice questions, 3 short answer questions, 1 document based questions, and 1 long answer question.

If your class is AP, please describe how the Honors and AP experience differs from a student's perspective.

10% curve and less writing assignments.

Please describe any additional information you would like to provide.
